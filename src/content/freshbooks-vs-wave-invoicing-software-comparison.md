## Pricing: Wave Wins (Obviously)

Wave's pricing is hard to beat because it's free. Forever. No trial periods, no gotchas. You get unlimited invoices, basic expense tracking, and financial reporting without paying a dime.

The catch? Wave makes money when you process payments (2.9% + 30¢ per transaction) and sells add-ons like payroll ($40/month) and payment processing upgrades.

FreshBooks starts at $17/month for up to 5 clients, jumping to $32 for 50 clients and $55 for 500 clients. Every plan includes time tracking, expense management, and project tools.

Here's the math: If you're processing $2,000 in monthly payments through Wave, you're paying $58 in fees. Add payroll and you're at $98/month. Suddenly FreshBooks doesn't look expensive.

**Winner: Wave** (for very small businesses)

## Core Features: FreshBooks Takes It

Wave handles basic invoicing well. You can create invoices, track when they're viewed, send reminders, and accept online payments. The accounting features are surprisingly solid for free software.

But FreshBooks feels like it was built for real businesses. The invoice editor is intuitive with drag-and-drop customization. Time tracking integrates perfectly with project management. Expense tracking includes receipt scanning through your phone camera.

FreshBooks also handles recurring invoices better. Wave can do them, but setting up complex billing cycles is clunky. FreshBooks makes it simple with templates for weekly, monthly, or custom schedules.

The project management tools in FreshBooks are genuinely useful. You can track time per project, set budgets, collaborate with team members, and generate detailed reports. Wave has basic project tracking but it feels like an afterthought.

**Winner: FreshBooks**

## Ease of Use: Close Call

Both tools are beginner-friendly, but they take different approaches.

Wave feels like Google Docs, clean and minimal. The dashboard shows your cash flow, recent invoices, and outstanding payments. Creating an invoice takes maybe 30 seconds once you're familiar with it.

FreshBooks has more visual polish but also more complexity. The dashboard is information-dense with widgets for everything. New users might feel overwhelmed, but power users love having everything accessible.

I prefer FreshBooks' invoice creation flow. The templates look more professional out of the box, and customizing them doesn't require design skills. Wave's templates work fine but look generic.

Mobile apps favor FreshBooks slightly. Both let you create invoices and track expenses on the go, but FreshBooks' app feels more responsive and includes better offline functionality.

**Winner: FreshBooks** (by a nose)

## Integrations: FreshBooks Dominates

This isn't close. FreshBooks integrates with over 100 apps including Shopify, Slack, Trello, and every major payment processor. The Zapier integration opens up hundreds more possibilities.

Wave connects to about 20 apps, mostly banking and payment tools. The selection covers basics like PayPal and Etsy, but you won't find CRM integrations or advanced automation options.

For solopreneurs, Wave's integrations might be enough. But if you're running multiple tools and want them to talk to each other, FreshBooks is the only real choice.

**Winner: FreshBooks**

## Customer Support: FreshBooks Wins Big

Wave offers email support. That's it. Response times average 24-48 hours, and the help articles are basic.

FreshBooks provides phone support during business hours, live chat, and comprehensive email support. I've used their phone support twice and got through to humans quickly both times. The help center is extensive with video tutorials and step-by-step guides.

This difference matters when you're dealing with accounting software. Tax questions, payment processing issues, and integration problems need real-time help.

**Winner: FreshBooks**

## Specific Use Cases

**Freelancers Just Starting Out**: Wave wins. You need basic invoicing without monthly fees eating into your profits. The free plan handles everything until you're making real money.

**Service-Based Businesses**: FreshBooks dominates. Time tracking, project management, and client collaboration tools justify the cost. The professional invoice templates also help with credibility.

**E-commerce Sellers**: Depends on volume. Wave works for occasional sales, but FreshBooks handles inventory tracking and integrates better with selling platforms.

**Growing Teams**: FreshBooks only. Wave doesn't handle multiple users well, and collaboration features are limited.

**International Businesses**: FreshBooks handles multi-currency invoicing and international tax requirements better.
