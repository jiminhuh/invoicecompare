## What Wave Actually Does

Wave is accounting software built for small businesses and freelancers. The core product includes invoicing, expense tracking, basic accounting, and financial reporting. Think of it as QuickBooks Lite, but actually free instead of "free trial then gotcha."

I started using Wave when my previous invoicing tool jacked up their prices. The onboarding was refreshingly simple. You connect your bank account, import some basic business info, and you're creating invoices within 10 minutes. No sales calls, no credit card required upfront.

The interface feels modern without being flashy. Everything sits where you'd expect it to. The invoice builder uses a clean drag-and-drop system, and you can customize templates without touching code.

## Core Features That Matter

### Invoicing

The invoicing system is Wave's strongest feature. You can create professional-looking invoices, set up recurring billing, and track payment status. The templates look good out of the box, though customization options are somewhat limited compared to [FreshBooks](https://example.com/go/freshbooks).

I particularly like the automatic payment reminders. You can set up sequences that gently nudge clients without being annoying. The system sends reminders at intervals you choose, and you can customize the tone.

One nice touch: Wave automatically saves draft invoices as you work. I've lost invoices in other tools when my browser crashed, but that's never happened here.

### Expense Tracking

Expense tracking works through bank connections and receipt uploads. You can snap photos of receipts with the mobile app, and Wave attempts to extract the key details. It's not perfect but gets the basics right most of the time.

The bank sync feature pulls in transactions automatically. You then categorize them as business expenses, personal expenses, or income. It's tedious at first but gets faster as Wave learns your patterns.

### Basic Accounting

Wave handles double-entry bookkeeping behind the scenes. You don't need to understand debits and credits, but the data is there if your accountant needs it. The system generates standard reports like profit and loss, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.

For simple businesses, this covers everything you need for tax time. I export my P&L statement at year-end and hand it to my accountant. She's never complained about the format.

### Payment Processing

This is where Wave makes money. They integrate with payment processors to let clients pay invoices directly. Credit card processing costs 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction. Bank transfers (ACH) cost 1% with a $1 minimum.

These rates aren't the cheapest available, but they're reasonable for the convenience. Clients can pay with one click from the invoice email. Money typically hits your account in 2-3 business days.

## How Wave Compares to Competitors

### Wave vs. FreshBooks

[FreshBooks](https://example.com/go/freshbooks) is more polished and feature-rich, but costs $17-50/month depending on your plan. FreshBooks has better time tracking, project management, and client portal features.

Wave wins on price (obviously) and simplicity. If you just need invoicing and basic bookkeeping, Wave does the job without monthly fees. FreshBooks makes sense if you need advanced project tracking or work with larger teams.

### Wave vs. QuickBooks

[QuickBooks](https://example.com/go/quickbooks) is the 800-pound gorilla of small business accounting. It's more powerful than Wave but costs $30-200/month. QuickBooks has better inventory management, advanced reporting, and integrations with other business tools.

Wave is perfect for service businesses that don't need inventory tracking. QuickBooks is overkill (and overpriced) unless you're running a more complex operation.

### Wave vs. Zoho Invoice

[Zoho Invoice](https://example.com/go/zoho) offers a free plan for up to 5 clients, then costs $10-40/month. Zoho has better automation and workflow features, plus it integrates with the broader Zoho ecosystem.

Wave's free plan has no client limits, making it better for freelancers with many small clients. Zoho wins if you're already using other Zoho products or need advanced automation.

### Wave vs. Square Invoices

[Square Invoices](https://example.com/go/square) is free for basic invoicing but limited in accounting features. Square's strength is payment processing (they're primarily a payments company).

Wave offers much better accounting and reporting. Square makes sense if you're already using Square for in-person payments and just need simple invoicing.

## What I Like About Wave

**Actually free.** This isn't a trial or freemium trap. The core features work without paying anything.

**Clean interface.** Everything feels intuitive. I didn't need tutorials to start creating invoices.

**Reliable bank sync.** My transactions import consistently without manual intervention.

## What Drives Me Crazy

**Limited customization.** Invoice templates look professional but you can't customize them extensively. The color scheme options are pretty basic.

**No time tracking.** This is a huge gap for consultants and agencies. You need a separate tool to track billable hours.

**Basic project management.** You can't organize invoices by project or track project profitability easily.

**Support limitations.** Free users get email support only. Response times can be slow during busy periods.

**Payment processing fees add up.** The 2.9% + 30¢ fee is reasonable but not the cheapest available. High-volume businesses might want to shop around.
